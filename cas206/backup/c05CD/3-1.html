Effectively Using the DIV tag with IDs

Step 1 - No styling yet. We use absolute positioning to create a two-column layout. This format allows you to follow the widely-adopted convention of placing navigation in a vertical "stack" on the left side.

The content area (the second column) needs to be much wider than the navigation column, as this is where the "real" information goes.

Step 2 - the two divs are aligned vertically. The navigation div (with its right borger in red so you can see where it is) has been absolutely positioned so its removed from the flow of the document.

As an absolutely positioned element, its default position is in the top left corner of its containing element: the body.

This allows the content div to take its place as the first element in the document flow.

By adding a left-margin to the content div that is equal to the width of the navigation, we have our two columns.

The next step is to do some styling and turn the page into a more professional looking layout. We will pad the content away from the edge of the divs, change the typeface, and make the navigation look a little more inviting.

Step 4 - cleanup complete. With all the functional issues resolved, we can turn off the styles we used to help us see what we were doing.

The background color is removed from the div, and the box around the h1 element is replaced with the same styles we used on the side columns, with a tiny bit of top padding to center the type between the two lines.

Adding text-align:center to the header div centers the h1 in older version of IE.
